Beyond Standard Assistive Devices: FlexADT, a Modular Toolkit for Assistive Technology Customization

Yunting Yan*, Gavin Chen*, Ruolin Ye*, Tapomayukh Bhattacharjee
Cornell University

Abstract

Millions of people rely on Assistive Technology Devices (ATDs) worldwide, yet ATDs often fail to meet care recipients’ (CRs') needs and leave caregivers (CG) with a substantial physical workload. To address this, we present FlexADT, a toolkit that transforms common ATDs into customizable instrumented agents. FlexADT provides modular plug-and-play hardware add-ons for sensing and actuation, paired with software that provides perception, planning, and control; both are designed for integration with powered wheelchairs, beds, and patient lifts. On top of this full-stack infrastructure, an LLM-powered interface leverages user functionality (e.g. range of motion), behavior (e.g. preferences), and environment (e.g. spatial layout) to recommend instrumentation for flexible, adaptive ATD customization. We evaluate FlexADT in two areas: (1) demonstrating adaptability across diverse CR personas and (2) comparing kit instrumentation versus standard ATDs in a bed-to-wheelchair transfer user study, where the instrumented ATDs reduced physical workload without increasing cognitive demand.
